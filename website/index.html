§ I · The Flow

A three-act play, performed daily.

i.

Hold   fn

A pill slides down from the notch. It is the only sign that anyone is listening, and it is gone the moment you finish.

ii.

Speak

Audio is captured locally and passed to whisper.cpp, accelerated on the Mac's own GPU. No network, no queue, no telemetry.

iii.

Paste

Cleaned text drops into whichever app held the cursor — your email, your editor, your terminal, your notes.

§ II · The Position

Local-first, by construction.

Murmur runs the entire transcription pipeline on your Mac. No socket opens during a dictation, no analytics ping is fired afterward, no account is required to install. The audio file lives for a few seconds inside ~/Library/Caches/Murmur and is removed the moment its transcript reaches the clipboard. The transcript itself is never written to any log. Murmur is MIT-licensed, so the only way to verify this is the only way that ever really matters: read the source.

Network
None during use. Sparkle requests an appcast only when checking for updates.
Telemetry
None. No analytics, no crash reports, no anonymous pings.

§ III · The Tools

Six surfaces, all yours to adjust.

  • 01

    Settings

    Seven tabs. Hotkey picker, profile picker, vocabulary editor, model chooser, update channel, and an honest About panel.

  • 02

    History

    Off by default — opt-in if you want a searchable record of past dictations, kept entirely on your machine.

  • 03

    Vocabulary

    Teach Murmur the words it keeps mishearing. Case-insensitive replacements, JSON import and export.

  • 04

    Profiles

    Raw, Casual, Formal, Code. Deterministic cleanup rules — no LLM, no surprise rewrites of what you said.

  • 05

    Models

    Pick a Whisper model that fits your Mac. Murmur downloads it with progress and verifies the SHA-256.

  • 06

    Updates

    Sparkle delivers signed updates from a public appcast. You stay in control of when to take them.

§ IV · The Reader

Made for people who write a lot, quietly.

i.

The writer

Drafts long emails, longer Notion pages, and the occasional essay. Wants to keep the cursor in flow without typing every word.

ii.

The coder

Dictates code comments and Slack replies into Cursor. The Code profile keeps spoken operators (equals equals, arrow) intact.

iii.

The researcher

Captures quotes and field notes in Obsidian. Murmur happily runs on a plane — there is no network step to fail.
