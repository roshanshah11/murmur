**Subject:** Murmur 1.0 — local-first dictation for the Mac (no cloud, no account)

Hi [FIRST NAME],

I shipped a Mac app this week and thought it might be up your alley. It's called Murmur — a dictation app that runs whisper.cpp on-device. Double-tap fn, speak, the text pastes into whatever app has focus. No account, no telemetry, no network calls during transcription. Free and MIT-licensed.

The short pitch: macOS dictation right now is split between Apple's stock (private, but context-blind) and cloud apps like Wispr Flow (sharp, but they stream your microphone audio to a server). Murmur is the middle path for writers and engineers who'd rather keep the audio on the machine, even if it costs a couple points of accuracy.

v1.0 ships with editable vocabulary, four cleanup profiles (Raw / Casual / Formal / Code), a whisper model picker from 75 MB through 2.9 GB, an opt-in history viewer, and Sparkle updates. macOS 13+, Apple Silicon and Intel.

I've attached the press kit (screenshots, icon, one-liner, longer blurb) and a single demo screenshot inline. Site is at https://murmur-landing-phi.vercel.app/, source at https://github.com/roshanshah11/murmur.
